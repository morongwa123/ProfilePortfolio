BCom Honours student at Wits University, studying Marketing with an Information Systems foundation.

Where I'm from

I'm from Polokwane, Limpopo, with strong ties to the local business community there through family. That grounding shaped how I think about business problems — practically, and with an eye for what actually helps the people running them.

What I studied

I completed my BCom in Information Systems at Wits, and I'm now doing my BComHons in Marketing. That combination means I approach marketing problems with a systems mindset, and I approach technical problems with a strategist's sense of what they're actually for.

Current research

I'm co-authoring an Honours research paper on hyper-personalisation and its impact on customer engagement and consumer-brand relationship quality, using Netflix subscribers in South Africa as the study population. It's given me a much sharper lens on where personalisation helps a brand and where it starts to feel invasive to the customer.

Where I'm headed

I'm drawn to work at the intersection of marketing, UX, application development and data analysis — roles where understanding both the business intent behind a decision and the technical reality of building it actually matters. I'm building toward that through projects like Bardeli and NDAMP, alongside self-teaching JavaScript and web development.

Outside of coursework

I'm a Class Representative for the BComHons Marketing cohort at Wits, liaising between students and faculty. I'm also self-teaching through FreeCodeCamp — certified in Responsive Web Design, currently working through JavaScript Algorithms and Data Structures.
